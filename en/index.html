üöÄ What is Microsoft 365 Copilot?

Microsoft 365 Copilot is an AI assistant integrated directly into the applications you already use ‚Äì Outlook, Teams, Word, Excel, PowerPoint and more. It works exclusively with data within your Microsoft 365 environment (emails, files on OneDrive/SharePoint, meetings, chats) and respects all permissions ‚Äì it can only see what you have access to.

At CULS you have access to M365 ‚Äì this guide will show you how Copilot can specifically help you in your role at FES.

üí¨ Copilot Chat (Central Interface)

The main entry point to the AI assistant. Available at m365.cloud.microsoft/chat or directly in Teams. Ask questions, work with documents and call specialised agents here.

Core Features

  • Work mode ‚Äì reads and works with organisational data: emails, files on OneDrive/SharePoint, calendar, Teams chats
  • Web mode ‚Äì searches the internet for information (academic sources, current trends)
  • Slash (/) ‚Äì reference a specific file, person, meeting, email or SharePoint directly in your prompt
  • At sign (@) ‚Äì invoke a specialised agent (Researcher, Analyst, etc.) directly in the conversation

Personalisation

  • Custom Instructions ‚Äì set up your profile: "I am a researcher in ecology, write in academic style, always in English"
  • Memory ‚Äì Copilot remembers important information: "Remember that I'm working on EU Horizon project‚Ä¶"
  • Prompt Gallery ‚Äì save frequently used prompts and share them with your department
  • Scheduled Prompts ‚Äì automatic execution (e.g. every Monday morning "summarise my new emails and tasks from last week")

Working with Content

  • Notebooks ‚Äì upload multiple documents for one project/grant and query across them
  • Pages ‚Äì collaborative text editing with AI (similar to Canvas in ChatGPT)
  • Export outputs ‚Äì from chat to Word, PowerPoint or Pages with one click

Output quality depends on input quality. Use the 4-question framework:

2Ô∏è‚É£
WHY

3Ô∏è‚É£

4Ô∏è‚É£

[WHY] I need it for an interim project report to the funding agency.

‚úÖ Getting Started ‚Äì Step by Step

1. Verify Access

  • Sign in to m365.cloud.microsoft/chat with your university account
  • If you see Copilot Chat ‚Äì you have access ‚úÖ
  • If not ‚Äì contact CULS IT department regarding licensing

2. Set Up Personalisation

  1. In Copilot Chat, click the settings icon (gear wheel)
  2. Go to Custom Instructions
  3. Fill in your profile: role, field, preferred language, communication style

3. Move Files to the Cloud

  • Copilot can only see OneDrive and SharePoint ‚Äì not your local disk!
  • Move key documents to OneDrive or your department/project SharePoint
  • Name files meaningfully (not "final_v3_revised2.docx")

4. Try Your First Prompts

  1. Open Copilot Chat ‚Üí "Summarise my unread emails this week"
  2. In Teams after a meeting ‚Üí click "Summarise" in Copilot
  3. In Word ‚Üí open a document and click the Copilot icon in the ribbon
  4. In Outlook ‚Üí when writing an email, click "Draft with Copilot"

5. Expand Gradually

  • Start with one application you use most frequently
  • After a week, add another
  • Save proven prompts to your Gallery
  • Share experiences with colleagues in your department
